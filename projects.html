Research Projects

Our lab is interested in uncovering how TRiC maintains protein homeostasis in cells. We investigate the molecular principles by which the human chaperonin TRiC/CCT assists protein folding and the conformational dynamics of TRiC itself. We integrate biochemistry, structural biology, and cell biology techniques including cryo-EM, cryo-ET, and mass spectrometry to address these questions. In a broader context, we also investigate how TRiC activity supports cellular function and stress resilience, how its dysregulation contributes to diseases, and how our mechanistic insights into TRiC mediated folding can guide the development of biologics and therapeutic strategies for cancer and neurodegenerative disorders.
