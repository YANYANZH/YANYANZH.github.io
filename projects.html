Research Projects

Our research focuses on understanding the fundamental principles of protein folding and applying these insights to develop innovative therapeutics. We combine structural biology, biochemistry, and computational approaches to address key questions in protein science and disease mechanisms.

Current Projects

Protein Folding Mechanisms

Active

We investigate the fundamental principles governing how proteins fold into their native three-dimensional structures. Using advanced biophysical techniques and computational modeling, we map energy landscapes and identify key intermediates in the folding pathway.

Techniques: Cryo-EM, NMR spectroscopy, molecular dynamics simulations, single-molecule FRET

Therapeutic Protein Design for Cancer

Active

Leveraging our understanding of protein folding principles, we design novel biologics targeting cancer-specific pathways. Our approach combines rational design with directed evolution to create highly specific and stable therapeutic proteins with enhanced efficacy.

Techniques: Protein engineering, structural biology, cell-based assays, in vivo models

Funding: [Grant information if applicable]

Protein Misfolding in Neurodegenerative Disorders

Active

We study the molecular mechanisms of protein misfolding and aggregation in neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. Our goal is to identify therapeutic targets and develop strategies to prevent or reverse pathological protein aggregation.

Techniques: Cryo-EM, biochemical assays, cellular models, structural analysis

Key Focus: Amyloid formation, aggregate structures, intervention strategies

Computational Prediction of Protein Structures

Active

We develop and apply computational methods to predict protein structures and folding pathways. By integrating experimental data with machine learning approaches, we aim to accelerate the discovery of novel protein folds and design principles.

Techniques: AlphaFold, molecular dynamics, deep learning, bioinformatics

Applications: Drug design, protein engineering, structure prediction
